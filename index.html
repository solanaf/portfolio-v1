About me

I am a Bioengineering master's student at U.C. San Diego pursuing research in the Ung Translational Neurophysiology lab. I work on the testing, development, and deployment of quantitative EEG and artificial intelligence tools, including transformer-based seizure detection, to improve the efficiency and efficacy of managing epilepsy in the inpatient setting.

In addition to my thesis, I work with other types of biological data, including RNA-seq, DNA-seq, and ATAC-seq. My interdisciplinary studies include, but are not limited to, neuroscience, computer science, AI, and bioinformatics.

In my free time, I implement data anlysis techniques for non-biological data.

What i'm doing

  • Neuroinformatics

    Using electrophysiology data from human EEG and non-human primate penetrative electrodes.

  • Bioinformatics

    Retrieving and processing raw FASTQ files, TCGA datasets using NIH SRA CLI toolkit, dimensionality reduction, identifying positively and negatively mutated genes, NMF for identifying meaningful features, survival analysis/models

  • Data Science & AI

    Providing insights into large-scale datasets of all types.

  • CAD & EDA

    Designed in SOLIDWORKS, I 3D-print using both SLA and FDM technologies. I also possess some experience building integrated circuits with Cadence Virtuoso.
